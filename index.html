Canada X Santa Catarina

Canada is a country that is in North America and is the second largest country in the world in terms of territorial extension, behind only Russia. It has rugged terrain and climates that vary from Temperate, further south, to Arctic, in the north The Canadian population today is just over 37.7 million inhabitants, who are concentrated in the southern portions of the territory.The country has high social and economic indicators, currently being one of the largest economies on the planet.

The discovery of the Canadian territories was made by the navigator John Cabot, from England, in 1497. Their colonization began with the French in 1554. Over the years there were many disputes over land between England, Canada and the thirteen colonies ( U.S).Due to these disputes, the Quebec Act was issued by the English, which allowed the French in Canada. Therefore, to this day we have reflections of disputes in today's society, such as the two official languages ​​(English and French) in the regions.

Canada is as big as it is inhospitable. They have the four seasons well defined. Thus, summer can register 35 ºC, while winter can register minus 50 ºC. The temperature fluctuation depends on the region. The climate limits the supply of arable land and there is little availability of crops in most of the territory. The territory is bathed by numerous rivers and crossed by several mountain ranges. Canada's temperate climate varies depending on the relief, precipitation and atmospheric pressure. The most temperate area is found on the coast of British Columbia. In this region, the influence of hot and humid air currents that leave the Pacific is intense. Snow is rare in this region. The warm, moist air coming off the Pacific Ocean is trapped in the Cordillera area, which forms the Coast Range and Rocky Mountains. Unable to advance towards the plains, the humid air crosses the mountains, where it cools and falls in the form of rain. Rain, however, is less in the valleys between the mountains and, therefore, there are high temperature summers.

With the discovery of the rich lands of Brazil, Santa Catarina soon entered the route of European navigators. The first significant expedition to reach Santa Catarina was that of the Portuguese Juan Dias Solis, in 1515. The coast of Santa Catarina was inhabited by Carijó Indians, from the Tupi-Guarani group. The navigator named the waters between Santa Catarina Island and the mainland “Bay of the Lost” due to the sinking of a vessel there. The Italian Sebastião Caboto, in the service of Spain, arrived with his expedition in 1526 and, when publishing his maps referring to the region, he called the Island of Santa Catarina “Porto dos Patos”. The name of Santa Catarina appears, for the first time, on Diego Ribeiro's world map, from 1529. There are disagreements as to who was responsible for the name of Santa Catarina: some authors attribute it to Sebastião Caboto, in honor of his wife, Catarina Medrano; others argue that it was in honor of Saint Catherine of Alexandria, celebrated by the Catholic church on November 25th. The State began to be truly populated in 1637, with the arrival of the bandeirantes, who began the future occupation of the capital, Nossa Senhora do Desterro, later named Florianópolis. In 1660, the village of Nossa Senhora da Graça, today São Francisco do Sul, was founded. In 1714, the second municipality of Santa Catarina was created, called Santo Antônio dos Anjos da Laguna, today known as Laguna. In 1739, Santa Catarina became the most advanced Portuguese post in South America, which was the ambition of the Spanish who planned to invade the island in 1777. The Spanish expelled troops and authorities to the mainland, and the island was returned to Portugal after the Treaty of Santo Idelfonso that same year.
